Diabetes Prevention and Prediabetes Management
Sources: Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) Study, ADA Standards of Care 2024, Finnish Diabetes Prevention Study, Da Qing Study, WHO

PREDIABETES - DEFINITION AND DIAGNOSIS

Prediabetes is a metabolic state between normal glucose tolerance and diabetes. It indicates elevated blood glucose levels that are above normal but below the diagnostic threshold for diabetes. Prediabetes is a critical window for intervention, as lifestyle changes can prevent or significantly delay progression to Type 2 diabetes.

Diagnostic Criteria:
- Impaired Fasting Glucose (IFG): Fasting plasma glucose 100-125 mg/dL (5.6-6.9 mmol/L)
- Impaired Glucose Tolerance (IGT): 2-hour OGTT glucose 140-199 mg/dL (7.8-11.0 mmol/L)
- HbA1c: 5.7-6.4% (39-47 mmol/mol)
- IFG + IGT combined confers highest risk of progression

EPIDEMIOLOGY

Global:
- An estimated 720 million adults worldwide have prediabetes (IDF 2021)
- Prevalence increasing rapidly, especially in low- and middle-income countries
- Without intervention, 5-10% of people with prediabetes progress to T2DM annually
- Up to 70% of people with prediabetes will eventually develop diabetes

Turkey:
- TURDEP-II study (2010): Prediabetes prevalence 30.8% in Turkish adults
- Combined with diabetes prevalence of 13.7%, nearly half of the adult population has dysglycemia
- Prevalence higher in women, urban populations, and those with obesity
- TEMD (Turkish Society of Endocrinology and Metabolism) recommends screening from age 40

Risk factors for progression:
- Higher baseline glucose levels (combined IFG + IGT has 60-70% 5-year conversion rate)
- Obesity (especially visceral/central obesity: waist >94cm men, >80cm women)
- Family history of T2DM
- History of gestational diabetes
- Physical inactivity
- Ethnicity (higher risk in South Asian, Middle Eastern, Hispanic populations)
- Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
- Sleep disorders (obstructive sleep apnea)

LANDMARK PREVENTION TRIALS

1. Diabetes Prevention Program (DPP) - USA, 2002:
- 3,234 participants with prediabetes, mean age 51, mean BMI 34
- Three arms: Intensive lifestyle intervention, metformin 850mg BID, placebo
- Results at 2.8 years:
  * Lifestyle intervention: 58% reduction in diabetes incidence
  * Metformin: 31% reduction
- Lifestyle intervention involved: 7% weight loss target, 150 min/week physical activity
- DPP Outcomes Study (15-year follow-up): Lifestyle group maintained 27% reduction, metformin group 18% reduction
- Cost-effective: Lifestyle intervention saves money long-term through prevented complications

2. Finnish Diabetes Prevention Study (DPS) - 2001:
- 522 overweight adults with IGT
- Individualized counseling on weight reduction, dietary fat, fiber, exercise
- Results: 58% reduction in diabetes risk (identical to DPP)
- Even modest weight loss (3-5%) significantly reduced risk
- Benefits persisted for 13+ years after active intervention ended

3. Da Qing Study - China, 1997 (30-year follow-up published 2019):
- 577 adults with IGT randomized to diet, exercise, diet + exercise, or control
- 6-year active intervention, 30-year follow-up
- Results: 39% reduction in diabetes incidence, 26% reduction in cardiovascular events, 33% reduction in all-cause mortality, delayed diabetes onset by average 3.96 years

4. Indian Diabetes Prevention Programme (IDPP) - 2006:
- Demonstrated effectiveness in Asian Indian population
- Lifestyle modification: 28.5% reduction
- Metformin: 26.4% reduction
- Important because it showed benefits in a lean population (BMI ~26) with high genetic susceptibility

LIFESTYLE INTERVENTIONS

Weight Management:
- Primary goal: 7% weight loss from baseline (DPP target)
- Even 3-5% weight loss provides meaningful metabolic improvement
- Weight loss reduces insulin resistance, hepatic fat, visceral fat
- Every 1 kg weight loss associated with 16% reduction in diabetes risk
- Sustained weight loss is more important than rapid weight loss
- Caloric deficit of 500-750 kcal/day recommended

Dietary Recommendations:
- Mediterranean diet: Strongest evidence for diabetes prevention (PREDIMED trial: 52% reduction with Mediterranean diet + extra virgin olive oil)
- DASH diet: Effective for both diabetes prevention and hypertension
- Key principles:
  * Reduce total caloric intake
  * Limit saturated fat to <10% of calories
  * Increase fiber (25-30 g/day): Whole grains, vegetables, legumes, fruits
  * Replace refined carbohydrates with complex carbohydrates
  * Include healthy fats: Olive oil, nuts, avocado, fatty fish
  * Limit processed meats and red meat
  * Reduce sugar-sweetened beverages (replaced with water, unsweetened tea/coffee)
  * Moderate alcohol consumption (if any): ≤1 drink/day for women, ≤2 for men
  * Coffee consumption (3-4 cups/day) associated with 25% lower diabetes risk

Physical Activity:
- ADA recommendation: 150 minutes/week moderate-intensity aerobic exercise (brisk walking, cycling, swimming)
- Alternative: 75 minutes/week vigorous-intensity activity (running, interval training)
- Resistance/strength training: 2-3 sessions per week (improves insulin sensitivity independent of aerobic exercise)
- Reduce prolonged sitting: Break up sedentary time every 30 minutes with light activity
- Exercise improves insulin sensitivity for 24-72 hours after activity
- Combined aerobic + resistance training is more effective than either alone

Sleep and Stress:
- Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night
- Short sleep (<6 hours) and poor sleep quality increase diabetes risk by 28%
- Screen for and treat obstructive sleep apnea
- Chronic stress management: Mindfulness, yoga, cognitive behavioral techniques
- Cortisol dysregulation from chronic stress promotes insulin resistance

PHARMACOLOGICAL PREVENTION

Metformin:
- Only medication with strong evidence for diabetes prevention
- ADA recommends considering metformin for prediabetes especially in:
  * BMI ≥35 kg/m²
  * Age <60 years
  * Women with prior gestational diabetes
  * Rising HbA1c despite lifestyle intervention
- Dose: 500-1700 mg/day
- DPP study: Most effective in younger, heavier individuals (BMI >35)

Other agents studied but not routinely recommended for prevention:
- Acarbose (STOP-NIDDM trial): 25% reduction, but GI side effects limit use
- Pioglitazone (ACT NOW trial): 72% reduction, but weight gain and fracture risk
- GLP-1 receptor agonists: SCALE Insulin Resistance study showed benefit, not yet approved for prevention
- SGLT2 inhibitors: Potential but not studied specifically for prevention
- Orlistat: Modest benefit through weight loss

SCREENING RECOMMENDATIONS

ADA recommends screening for prediabetes/diabetes:
- All adults aged ≥35 years
- Any age if BMI ≥25 (≥23 in Asian Americans) with one or more risk factors:
  * Physical inactivity
  * First-degree relative with diabetes
  * High-risk ethnicity
  * History of GDM or baby >4 kg
  * Hypertension (≥140/90 or on treatment)
  * HDL <35 mg/dL or triglycerides >250 mg/dL
  * PCOS
  * HbA1c ≥5.7%, IFG, or IGT on previous testing
  * Conditions associated with IR (severe obesity, acanthosis nigricans)
  * History of CVD
- Repeat every 3 years if normal, annually if prediabetes
