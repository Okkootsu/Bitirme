HbA1c and Blood Glucose Monitoring in Diabetes
Sources: ADA Standards of Care 2024, International Consensus on CGM, AACE Guidelines

HEMOGLOBIN A1c (HbA1c)

Definition and Mechanism:
HbA1c (glycated hemoglobin) measures the percentage of hemoglobin that has glucose attached to it. Since red blood cells have a lifespan of approximately 120 days, HbA1c reflects average blood glucose levels over the preceding 2-3 months, with a greater weighting toward the most recent 30 days.

HbA1c Interpretation:
- Normal: <5.7% (39 mmol/mol)
- Prediabetes: 5.7-6.4% (39-47 mmol/mol)
- Diabetes diagnosis: ≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol)

Estimated Average Glucose (eAG) Correlation:
- HbA1c 5.0% → eAG 97 mg/dL (5.4 mmol/L)
- HbA1c 6.0% → eAG 126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L)
- HbA1c 7.0% → eAG 154 mg/dL (8.6 mmol/L)
- HbA1c 8.0% → eAG 183 mg/dL (10.2 mmol/L)
- HbA1c 9.0% → eAG 212 mg/dL (11.8 mmol/L)
- HbA1c 10.0% → eAG 240 mg/dL (13.4 mmol/L)

Glycemic Targets (ADA 2024):
- Most non-pregnant adults: HbA1c <7.0%
- If achievable without significant hypoglycemia: HbA1c <6.5%
- Older adults or those with comorbidities: HbA1c <8.0%
- Pregnancy (pre-existing diabetes): HbA1c <6.0-6.5%

Limitations of HbA1c:
- Does not capture glycemic variability (swings between highs and lows)
- Conditions that affect RBC turnover alter results:
  * Falsely low: Hemolytic anemia, chronic kidney disease (EPO therapy), blood transfusions, iron deficiency treatment, pregnancy (2nd/3rd trimester)
  * Falsely high: Iron deficiency anemia, vitamin B12 deficiency, splenectomy, alcoholism
- Hemoglobin variants (HbS, HbC, HbE) may interfere with some assay methods
- Does not reflect hypoglycemia burden

Monitoring Frequency:
- At least twice per year if meeting glycemic targets
- Quarterly (every 3 months) if therapy changed or not meeting targets

SELF-MONITORING OF BLOOD GLUCOSE (SMBG)

Indications:
- All patients on insulin therapy
- Patients on sulfonylureas or meglitinides (hypoglycemia risk)
- Patients not meeting glycemic targets
- During illness, stress, or medication changes
- Pregnancy with diabetes

Testing Frequency:
- Multiple daily insulin injections or pump: 4-10 times/day (before meals, 2h after meals, bedtime, before exercise, when symptoms suggest hypoglycemia)
- Basal insulin only: At minimum, fasting glucose daily
- Non-insulin therapy: Individualized; may be helpful for monitoring patterns

Blood Glucose Targets:
- Fasting/pre-meal: 80-130 mg/dL (4.4-7.2 mmol/L)
- 2-hour postprandial: <180 mg/dL (10.0 mmol/L)
- Bedtime: 90-150 mg/dL

CONTINUOUS GLUCOSE MONITORING (CGM)

Technology Types:
1. Real-time CGM (rtCGM): Continuous display of glucose, alerts for highs/lows
   - Examples: Dexcom G7, Medtronic Guardian 4, Abbott FreeStyle Libre 3
   - Measures interstitial glucose every 1-5 minutes
   - Can alert for predicted highs and lows

2. Intermittently scanned CGM (isCGM / Flash):
   - Example: Abbott FreeStyle Libre 2
   - Requires scanning the sensor to see glucose data
   - Stores up to 8 hours of data

Key CGM Metrics (International Consensus 2019):
- Time in Range (TIR): 70-180 mg/dL → Target: >70% (≈HbA1c <7%)
- Time Below Range (TBR):
  * <70 mg/dL (Level 1 hypoglycemia): Target <4%
  * <54 mg/dL (Level 2 hypoglycemia): Target <1%
- Time Above Range (TAR):
  * >180 mg/dL (Level 1 hyperglycemia): Target <25%
  * >250 mg/dL (Level 2 hyperglycemia): Target <5%
- Glucose Management Indicator (GMI): Estimated HbA1c from CGM data
- Coefficient of Variation (CV): Measure of glycemic variability, target <36%

Benefits of CGM:
- Reduces HbA1c by 0.3-0.5% compared to SMBG alone
- Reduces hypoglycemia (especially nocturnal)
- Improves Time in Range
- Better understanding of glucose patterns (dawn phenomenon, postprandial spikes, exercise effects)
- Ambulatory Glucose Profile (AGP) provides standardized visualization

HYPOGLYCEMIA

Classification:
- Level 1: Glucose 54-70 mg/dL (alert value)
- Level 2: Glucose <54 mg/dL (clinically significant)
- Level 3: Severe event requiring assistance from another person

Symptoms:
- Adrenergic: Tremor, palpitations, sweating, anxiety, hunger, tingling
- Neuroglycopenic: Confusion, difficulty speaking, blurred vision, drowsiness, seizures, loss of consciousness
- Hypoglycemia unawareness: Loss of warning symptoms with recurrent hypoglycemia (affects 20-40% of T1DM patients)

Treatment (Rule of 15):
1. Consume 15-20g of fast-acting carbohydrate (glucose tablets, juice, regular soda)
2. Recheck glucose after 15 minutes
3. Repeat if still <70 mg/dL
4. Once normalized, eat a meal or snack to prevent recurrence
5. For severe hypoglycemia: Glucagon injection (1mg IM/SC) or nasal glucagon (Baqsimi 3mg)

HYPERGLYCEMIA

Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA):
- Primarily in Type 1, but can occur in Type 2 (ketosis-prone diabetes)
- Criteria: Blood glucose >250 mg/dL, pH <7.3, bicarbonate <18 mEq/L, positive ketones
- Treatment: IV fluids, insulin infusion, electrolyte replacement (potassium), treat underlying cause

Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State (HHS):
- Primarily in Type 2 diabetes
- Extreme hyperglycemia (often >600 mg/dL), severe dehydration, altered mental status
- Serum osmolality >320 mOsm/kg, minimal or no ketosis
- High mortality rate (10-20%)
- Treatment: Aggressive IV fluid resuscitation, insulin, electrolyte monitoring
