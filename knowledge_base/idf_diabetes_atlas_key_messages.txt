IDF Diabetes Atlas 10th Edition - Key Messages
Source: International Diabetes Federation (IDF), 2021

GLOBAL DIABETES STATISTICS

1. Approximately 537 million adults (20-79 years) are living with diabetes. This number is predicted to rise to 643 million by 2030 and 783 million by 2045.

2. Over 3 in 4 adults with diabetes live in low- and middle-income countries.

3. Diabetes caused 6.7 million deaths in 2021 – 1 every 5 seconds.

4. Diabetes caused at least USD 966 billion in health expenditure – a 316% increase over the last 15 years.

5. 541 million adults have Impaired Glucose Tolerance (IGT), which places them at high risk of type 2 diabetes.

6. Over 1.2 million children and adolescents (0-19 years) live with type 1 diabetes.

7. 1 in 6 live births (21 million) are affected by diabetes in pregnancy.

8. Over 3 in 4 people with diabetes live in low- and middle-income countries.

REGIONAL FINDINGS

The Middle East and North Africa (MENA) region has the highest age-adjusted comparative diabetes prevalence at 16.2%. The South-East Asia region has the largest number of adults living with diabetes at 90 million.

PREVENTION OF TYPE 2 DIABETES

Evidence from several countries has demonstrated that type 2 diabetes can be prevented or delayed through lifestyle modifications. Key interventions include:

- Weight management: Achieving and maintaining a healthy body weight (BMI < 25 kg/m²)
- Physical activity: At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity physical activity per week
- Healthy eating: A balanced diet rich in whole grains, fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins
- Smoking cessation: Tobacco use increases the risk of type 2 diabetes
- Regular screening: Early detection in high-risk populations

EARLY DETECTION AND MANAGEMENT

Early detection is essential for the effective management of diabetes. Approximately 240 million people, or about 1 in 2 adults currently living with diabetes, are undiagnosed. Most of these have type 2 diabetes.

Key recommendations for management:
- Self-monitoring of blood glucose
- Regular HbA1c testing (target < 7% for most adults)
- Blood pressure control (target < 130/80 mmHg)
- Lipid management
- Regular foot examinations
- Annual eye examinations
- Kidney function monitoring

DIABETES AND COVID-19

People with diabetes are at higher risk of severe illness from COVID-19. Ensuring access to diabetes care and maintaining glycemic control remains critical during the pandemic and beyond.
