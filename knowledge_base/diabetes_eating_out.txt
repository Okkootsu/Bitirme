Diabetes-Friendly Eating Out Guide
Source: American Diabetes Association; Diabetes UK; Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics

GENERAL STRATEGIES FOR DINING OUT

Eating out does not have to mean losing blood sugar control. With planning and smart choices, people with diabetes can enjoy restaurant meals safely. Studies show that frequent dining out is associated with higher calorie intake, larger portions, and increased HbA1c, but informed choices can minimize these effects.

Before you go:
- Check the restaurant menu online and plan your order in advance
- Do not skip meals earlier in the day to save calories (leads to overeating)
- Take medications at your usual time
- If eating later than normal, have a small healthy snack to prevent hypoglycemia
- Bring glucose tablets in case of delayed food service

PORTION CONTROL

Restaurant portions are typically 2-3 times larger than recommended serving sizes. Strategies:
- Ask for a smaller portion or half-size meal
- Request a to-go box at the start and set aside half
- Share entrees with a dining companion
- Use the plate method mentally: half vegetables, quarter protein, quarter starch
- Avoid all-you-can-eat buffets (very difficult to control portions)
- Order appetizer-sized portions as your main course

MENU NAVIGATION

Best cooking methods: Grilled, baked, steamed, broiled, poached, roasted.
Avoid or limit: Fried, breaded, battered, creamy, au gratin, smothered.

Appetizers: Choose salads (dressing on the side), broth-based soups, grilled vegetables. Avoid bread baskets, fried appetizers, cream soups.

Main courses: Grilled fish or chicken, lean steak, kebabs. Ask for vegetables instead of fries or rice. Request sauces on the side (many contain hidden sugar).

Side dishes: Steamed vegetables, side salad, grilled asparagus. Avoid mashed potatoes, french fries, white rice, creamy coleslaw.

Desserts: Fresh fruit, sugar-free options if available. Share a dessert if you want to indulge. Dark chocolate (small piece) has lower glycemic impact.

TURKISH RESTAURANT GUIDE

Healthy choices in Turkish cuisine:
- Grilled meats: Izgara kofte, tavuk sis, Adana kebab (watch portion size)
- Salads: Coban salata, piyaz, ezme (no added sugar)
- Soups: Mercimek corbasi (lentil soup, high fiber, good choice)
- Mezes: Haydari, atom, acili ezme (small portions)
- Avoid or limit: Pide, lahmacun (high carb), borek (pastry), baklava, kunefe

Beverages: Water, ayran (unsweetened), unsweetened tea. Avoid: sugary ayran, fruit juices, sodas, serbet.

FAST FOOD STRATEGIES

If fast food is the only option:
- Choose grilled over fried (grilled chicken sandwich over fried)
- Skip the bun or eat open-faced to reduce carbs
- Choose salad as a side instead of fries
- Water or unsweetened beverages
- Avoid supersizing
- Check nutrition information (most chains provide it online)
- Best fast food options: grilled chicken salad, plain hamburger (small), grilled chicken wrap

ALCOHOL WITH MEALS

- Alcohol can lower blood sugar, especially on an empty stomach
- Always eat food with alcohol
- Limit to 1 drink for women, 2 for men per sitting
- Dry wine and light beer have fewer carbs than cocktails
- Check blood sugar before bed after drinking
