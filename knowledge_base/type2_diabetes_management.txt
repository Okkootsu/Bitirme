Type 2 Diabetes Management Guidelines
Source: ADA Standards of Medical Care 2023; EASD/ADA Consensus Report

GLYCEMIC TARGETS

General adult targets:
- HbA1c: < 7.0% (53 mmol/mol) for most non-pregnant adults
- Preprandial (fasting) glucose: 80-130 mg/dL (4.4-7.2 mmol/L)
- Postprandial glucose (1-2 hours after meal): < 180 mg/dL (10.0 mmol/L)

Individualized targets:
- Stricter (HbA1c < 6.5%): Younger patients, short duration of diabetes, no cardiovascular disease
- Less strict (HbA1c < 8.0%): Elderly, long duration, history of severe hypoglycemia, limited life expectancy, advanced complications

PHARMACOLOGICAL MANAGEMENT

First-line therapy:
- Metformin remains the preferred initial oral medication
- Benefits: Weight neutral or modest weight loss, cardiovascular benefits, low cost
- Start at 500 mg once or twice daily, titrate to 1000 mg twice daily
- Main side effects: GI discomfort (usually transient), vitamin B12 deficiency
- Contraindicated in severe renal impairment (eGFR < 30 mL/min)

Second-line agents (based on patient characteristics):
- SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin): Preferred if cardiovascular disease or heart failure present. Benefits: weight loss, cardiovascular and renal protection.
- GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, liraglutide): Preferred if obesity or cardiovascular disease. Benefits: significant weight loss, cardiovascular protection.
- DPP-4 inhibitors (sitagliptin, linagliptin): Weight neutral, well tolerated, suitable for elderly.
- Thiazolidinediones (pioglitazone): Insulin sensitizer, but associated with weight gain and fluid retention.
- Sulfonylureas (glimepiride, gliclazide): Effective but risk of hypoglycemia and weight gain.
- Insulin therapy: When oral agents insufficient or severe hyperglycemia at diagnosis.

SELF-MONITORING OF BLOOD GLUCOSE (SMBG)

Recommended for patients on:
- Insulin therapy: Check 4-10 times daily (before meals, bedtime, before exercise)
- Oral medications with hypoglycemia risk: Check before meals and when symptomatic
- Non-insulin therapy: Periodic monitoring may be helpful

Continuous Glucose Monitoring (CGM):
- Time in Range (TIR): Target > 70% time between 70-180 mg/dL
- Time Below Range: < 4% time below 70 mg/dL
- Time Above Range: < 25% time above 180 mg/dL

CARDIOVASCULAR RISK MANAGEMENT

Blood pressure:
- Target: < 130/80 mmHg for most adults with diabetes
- First-line: ACE inhibitors or ARBs (especially with albuminuria)
- Lifestyle modifications: sodium restriction, DASH diet, weight loss, exercise

Lipids:
- Statin therapy recommended for all adults with diabetes aged 40-75
- High-intensity statin for those with ASCVD or 10-year ASCVD risk ≥ 20%
- LDL-C target: < 70 mg/dL for those with ASCVD

Antiplatelet therapy:
- Aspirin (75-162 mg/day) for secondary prevention in those with ASCVD
- May be considered for primary prevention in those with increased cardiovascular risk

DIABETES SELF-MANAGEMENT EDUCATION AND SUPPORT (DSMES)

Key components:
- Understanding the disease and treatment options
- Healthy eating and meal planning
- Physical activity incorporation
- Medication adherence and management
- Blood glucose monitoring and interpretation
- Problem-solving skills for daily challenges
- Psychosocial adaptation and coping
- Foot care and complication prevention

REGULAR MONITORING SCHEDULE

- HbA1c: Every 3-6 months
- Blood pressure: Every visit
- Lipid panel: Annually (more often if adjusting therapy)
- Kidney function (eGFR, urine albumin): Annually
- Dilated eye exam: Annually (or every 2 years if no retinopathy)
- Comprehensive foot exam: Annually
- Dental exam: Every 6 months
- Depression screening: Annually
