Diabetes Pharmacotherapy - Medication Classes and Guidelines
Sources: ADA Standards of Care 2024, EASD Consensus Report 2022, WHO Essential Medicines List

OVERVIEW OF DIABETES MEDICATIONS

The goal of pharmacotherapy in Type 2 diabetes is to achieve and maintain glycemic targets while minimizing side effects, particularly hypoglycemia and weight gain. Treatment should be individualized based on patient characteristics, comorbidities, and preferences.

FIRST-LINE THERAPY

Metformin (Biguanide):
- Mechanism: Reduces hepatic glucose production, improves insulin sensitivity, decreases intestinal absorption of glucose
- Typical dose: 500-2000 mg/day (start low, titrate slowly)
- HbA1c reduction: 1.0-1.5%
- Advantages: Weight neutral or modest weight loss, low hypoglycemia risk, cardiovascular benefit (UKPDS study), low cost, long safety record
- Side effects: GI symptoms (nausea, diarrhea, metallic taste) in 20-30% of patients, vitamin B12 deficiency with long-term use
- Contraindications: eGFR <30 mL/min, acute conditions with risk of lactic acidosis (sepsis, shock, hepatic failure)
- First-line for most patients with Type 2 diabetes regardless of HbA1c level

SECOND-LINE AND ADD-ON THERAPIES

1. GLP-1 Receptor Agonists (Incretin Mimetics):
- Examples: Semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy), Liraglutide (Victoza), Dulaglutide (Trulicity), Exenatide (Byetta)
- Mechanism: Mimic GLP-1 hormone - stimulate insulin secretion, suppress glucagon, slow gastric emptying, promote satiety
- HbA1c reduction: 1.0-1.8%
- Advantages: Significant weight loss (3-7 kg), cardiovascular benefit (LEADER, SUSTAIN-6, REWIND trials), low hypoglycemia risk
- Side effects: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (usually transient), injection site reactions, rare pancreatitis risk
- Preferred add-on for patients with established cardiovascular disease or high CV risk
- Preferred for patients who need weight loss

2. SGLT2 Inhibitors (Gliflozins):
- Examples: Empagliflozin (Jardiance), Dapagliflozin (Forxiga), Canagliflozin (Invokana), Ertugliflozin
- Mechanism: Block glucose reabsorption in proximal tubule of kidney, causing glycosuria (glucose excretion in urine)
- HbA1c reduction: 0.5-1.0%
- Advantages: Weight loss (2-3 kg), blood pressure reduction (3-5 mmHg systolic), cardiovascular benefit (EMPA-REG, CANVAS, DECLARE trials), renal protection (DAPA-CKD, CREDENCE trials), heart failure benefit
- Side effects: Genital mycotic infections (yeast infections), urinary tract infections, volume depletion, rare euglycemic DKA, Fournier's gangrene (very rare)
- Preferred for patients with heart failure or chronic kidney disease (eGFR >20 mL/min)
- Contraindicated in Type 1 diabetes (off-label use carries DKA risk)

3. DPP-4 Inhibitors (Gliptins):
- Examples: Sitagliptin (Januvia), Vildagliptin (Galvus), Saxagliptin, Linagliptin (Tradjenta), Alogliptin
- Mechanism: Inhibit DPP-4 enzyme that degrades endogenous GLP-1 and GIP, thereby increasing incretin levels
- HbA1c reduction: 0.5-0.8%
- Advantages: Weight neutral, low hypoglycemia risk, oral administration, well tolerated, no dose adjustment needed for linagliptin in renal impairment
- Side effects: Generally well tolerated, rare pancreatitis, joint pain, possible heart failure risk with saxagliptin (SAVOR-TIMI 53 trial)
- Useful when GLP-1 RA is not feasible (cost, injection aversion)

4. Sulfonylureas:
- Examples: Glimepiride, Gliclazide, Glipizide, Glyburide
- Mechanism: Stimulate insulin secretion from pancreatic beta cells by closing K-ATP channels
- HbA1c reduction: 1.0-1.5%
- Advantages: Low cost, rapid onset, effective
- Disadvantages: Hypoglycemia risk (especially glyburide), weight gain (2-4 kg), possible cardiovascular concerns, beta cell exhaustion over time
- Best choice: Gliclazide MR (lower hypoglycemia risk than glyburide, ADVANCE trial)
- Still widely used in low-resource settings due to cost

5. Thiazolidinediones (TZDs):
- Examples: Pioglitazone, Rosiglitazone
- Mechanism: Activate PPARγ receptors, improving insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, muscle, and liver
- HbA1c reduction: 1.0-1.5%
- Advantages: Durable glycemic effect, low hypoglycemia risk, pioglitazone has cardiovascular benefit (PROactive trial), beneficial effect on NAFLD
- Disadvantages: Weight gain, fluid retention, heart failure risk, bone fractures (especially in women), bladder cancer concern with pioglitazone (debated)
- Contraindicated in heart failure (NYHA Class III-IV)

6. Insulin Therapy:
- Required when oral agents fail to maintain glycemic control
- Types:
  * Basal insulin: Glargine (Lantus, Toujeo), Detemir (Levemir), Degludec (Tresiba), NPH
  * Prandial (bolus) insulin: Lispro (Humalog), Aspart (NovoRapid), Glulisine (Apidra)
  * Premixed: 70/30 (NPH/Regular), BiAsp 30 (NovoMix 30)
- Starting dose for basal insulin: 10 units or 0.1-0.2 units/kg/day
- Titrate by 2-4 units every 3-7 days based on fasting glucose

TREATMENT ALGORITHM (ADA/EASD 2022 Consensus)

Step 1: Lifestyle modification + Metformin (for all patients)

Step 2: Add second agent based on patient profile:
- If atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD): GLP-1 RA or SGLT2i (with proven CV benefit)
- If heart failure: SGLT2i (empagliflozin or dapagliflozin)
- If chronic kidney disease: SGLT2i (if eGFR adequate) or GLP-1 RA
- If weight loss is priority: GLP-1 RA > SGLT2i
- If cost is major barrier: Sulfonylurea (gliclazide) or TZD (pioglitazone)
- If hypoglycemia is major concern: GLP-1 RA, SGLT2i, DPP-4i, or TZD

Step 3: Triple therapy or insulin initiation if targets not met

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

- Ramadan fasting: Diabetes patients who fast during Ramadan should be risk-stratified. Pre-Ramadan education, medication adjustment (dose timing, drug selection), and glucose monitoring are essential. IDF-DAR Practical Guidelines provide specific recommendations.
- Elderly patients (≥65 years): Less stringent HbA1c targets (7.5-8.0%), avoid sulfonylureas and complex insulin regimens, prioritize safety over tight control
- Renal impairment: Adjust metformin dose, prefer SGLT2i for renal protection, GLP-1 RA safe in most stages, avoid glyburide
- Pregnancy: Only insulin is approved first-line; metformin may be used as alternative
